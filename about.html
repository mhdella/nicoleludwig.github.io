About

I am a PhD student in Energy Informatics at the Institute of Automation and Applied Informatics under the supervision of Prof. Veit Hagenmeyer and Prof. Ralf Mikut at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology.
I am also part of the research training group "Energystatusdata", funded by the German Research Foundation (DFG).

My PhD thesis focuses on methods to foster a more flexible energy demand. I am especially looking at data-driven methods to detect flexibility (flexibility mining) and market-based solutions to enhance the use of existing flexibility in the grid.

My research interests include machine learning, forecasting, decision science and Bayesian statistics with applications in economics and energy systems.

I received my Bachelor's degree in Economics (with a focus on Econometrics) and Master's degree in Information System and Network Economics from the University of Freiburg (Germany). I also spent a semester at the computer science and economics faculties of the University of Oslo.
Before coming to Karlsruhe, I worked at the Chair for Information Systems Research at the University of Freiburg, where my main research focus has been forecasting the electricity spot price.
